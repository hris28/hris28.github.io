# OSINT, an orientation

Open Source Intelligence, OSINT for short, is the practice of collecting and analyzing
information from publicly available sources to answer a specific question. The word
*intelligence* matters here. OSINT is not just search. It is directed, purposeful, and
usually written down.

## What OSINT is not

- Not hacking. If you need credentials to access it, it is not open source.
- Not a tool. Maltego and Shodan are tools, but the discipline is methodological.
- Not passive. Good OSINT asks sharp questions first, then collects.

## A starting loop

1. **Define the question.** What do I need to know, and why?
2. **Plan sources.** Which kinds of records would answer this?
3. **Collect, narrowly.** Avoid data hoarding. It costs time later.
4. **Verify.** At least two independent sources per claim.
5. **Document.** Who said what, where, when.

## Where the ethics come in

Publicly accessible and ethically usable are not the same set. A person's leaked
medical record is technically findable; using it is still wrong. The rule I am building
toward is: would this use survive the test of the person finding out about it?
