This is a space for thinking out loud. Some of what you'll find here
is polished writing; most is closer to lab notes — ideas caught before
they disappear, half-formed arguments, reading notes that might
eventually become essays.

## How it's organised

There are no strict categories. Notes link to each other directly, and
every page lists its **backlinks** at the bottom — the other notes that
mention it. Over time, those backlinks become a map.

The sidebar has a search box; that's usually the fastest way to find
something. Tags at the top of each note are another way in.

## What notes look like

A finished note has a title, a date, and usually a few paragraphs of
prose. A seedling note might be three sentences and a link. Both are
fine.

When something is clearly unfinished I'll say so at the top. When I
change my mind about something, I'll leave the old version visible
underneath.

> Notes are not a performance. They're closer to thinking in public —
> which is slower than thinking in private, but the revisions stick.

## How to link between notes

Inside a note, `[[double-bracket-wikilinks]]` link to other notes by
filename. For example, [[colophon]] explains how this site is built.

If a link points to a note that doesn't exist yet, that's fine — it
shows up as broken, and the broken links are a to-do list.

## What I'll probably write about

HCI and UX research, neural signal processing, machine learning papers
I'm reading, tools and workflows, things from my graduate coursework
that feel worth remembering. Occasionally something outside all of
that.
