Introduction

Hello — I'm Hrishika Roychoudhury, a graduate student in Information Science (MSIS) at UNC Chapel Hill's School of Information and Library Science. I finished my B.S. in Information Science and Neuroscience — with a minor in Computer Science — in December 2025, and I'm continuing through UNC's BS-MS Dual Degree Program. With passions across many disciplines, I try to take an interdisciplinary approach to whatever I commit to, and have been lucky to gain experience across a range of fields because of it.

My work sits at the intersection of human-computer interaction, neural networks, and human-centered design — machine-learning model evaluation, data visualisation, full-stack web development, and translating technical insight into design decisions people can actually use.

Values & passions

I love science. As a child I was the kid who wanted to know how birds fly, how plants grow, how people become sick, how animals communicate — and I'd ask every parent and teacher within reach. In school I found that science had answers to those questions, and that's how it started. From there it was science fairs, hands-on projects, and a long-running obsession with using what's around you to solve ordinary problems.

I'm deeply passionate about STEM education. I co-founded and led the Girls Who Code chapters at both my high school and at UNC, including founding UNC's GWC College Loop in 2026. I believe information is the key to almost everything: activism, agency, better decisions. Even in an era defined by information, too many people live without it. Given the right resources and some care, I think we can build a more informed society — and that's where the work starts.

Outside of STEM, I'm drawn to music and theatre. You can usually find me at a piano, writing lyrics, or choreographing. I've trained in Bharatanatyam for over eight years and dabbled in hip-hop and a few other forms — dance is in my bones, and I'm almost always tapping a foot to something. I also act: Shakespeare competitions, speech and debate, and anything else that lets me play a character for a while.

Activities & accolades

Research & competitions. I've pursued science research for over seven years, presenting on biotechnology and nanoparticles at the International Science and Engineering Fair (ISEF 2019), the American Junior Academy of Science (AJAS 2020, 2021), and as a finalist at the International BioGENEius Challenge (2020). I was selected as a Regeneron Science Talent Search Scholar — top 300 of 2,162 applicants (2022) — and a Coca-Cola Scholar Semifinalist (top 2% of 68,000+ applicants, 2021). Most recently, my Pearl Hacks hackathon project took the Highest STEM-Interest Inclusivity Runner-Up award from Qorvo Inc. (2023).

Entrepreneurship. I'm a co-founder and former COO of SkyeLabs Innovation Inc., where we pioneered EcoGel — a sustainable, mechanically superior biopolymer aerogel with applications in high-performance apparel, aerospace, and space exploration. We took the top honour, Pete Conrad Scholar, at the 2021 Conrad Spirit of Innovation Challenge, were a Top 10 Finalist and sole Honourable Mention at NASA iTech Cycle 1 (as the youngest and first-ever high school team to present), filed a provisional patent, and secured $2,000 in research grant funding from the Bowman-Brockman Endowment.

Leadership & service at UNC. I serve as a SILS Ambassador and on the SILS New School Student Advisory Board during the SILS–SDSS merger forming the School of Data and Information Sciences. I'm Co-President of UNC Zero Degrees (200+ members), and Facilitator and College Loop President for Girls Who Code at UNC — where I've taught Python to 50+ students weekly since 2023. Earlier, I served as a Girls Who Code × Pluralsight apprentice (handpicked as closing ceremony speaker) and as Editor-in-Chief of the Broad Street Scientific, NCSSM's journal of student STEM research.
