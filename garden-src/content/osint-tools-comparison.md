# OSINT Tools Comparison

Open-source intelligence, or OSINT, involves collecting and analyzing information from publicly available sources. OSINT should be practiced ethically, legally, and with respect for privacy.

## Tool comparison

| Tool | Purpose | Best for | Caution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Google advanced search | Search operators and targeted discovery | Quick public web research | Results can be incomplete or personalized |
| Internet Archive Wayback Machine | Historical webpage snapshots | Finding older versions of pages | Not every page is archived |
| Shodan | Internet-connected device search | Exposure discovery and asset awareness | Do not probe systems without authorization |
| Have I Been Pwned | Breach exposure lookup | Checking whether emails/passwords appear in known breaches | Use responsibly; do not test other people’s accounts |
| VirusTotal | File, URL, domain, and hash reputation | Malware and suspicious URL triage | Uploaded files may be shared with security partners |
| SecurityHeaders.com | HTTP security header checks | Website hardening review | Automated checks need human interpretation |

## Responsible OSINT principles

- Do not bypass authentication.
- Do not exploit systems.
- Do not harass or dox people.
- Do not collect more personal data than necessary.
- Document sources and methods.
- Respect platform terms and applicable law.
- When in doubt, stop and ask for guidance.
